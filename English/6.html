6. Development

I consider the documents preceding this one as the basis for reform of the Polish written language. Here, in turn, I made a list of optional changes, mostly cosmetic.

  1. No obligation to write...
    • uppercase letters. They will be available for non-language applications.
    • a dot . over letters I i, J j in handwritten form. In print and display they do not change due to their everlasting form in the basic Latin alphabet.
    • any character at the end of a bullet and table items.
    • any dots . or similar signs in a quick handwritten abbreviation. However, an underscore _ is a more comfortable choice instead of a dot.
  2. An obligation to write...
    • a dot after each letter of the acronym when readability is a priority.
    • particles nie, by combined with words within all parts of speech, but all other particles – separately.
    • punctuation marks inside quotation marks when using quotation marks.
    • the unit of measurement without a space after the numerical value.
    • a dot . or underscore _ after the ordinal value.
    • repeat a character at the beginning of the next line when breaking words and phrases to a new line.
    • uppercase letters in abbreviations when uppercase letters are used.
    • particles czy and similar starting questions in press titles. A question mark at the end of a title is weaker than the inverse mark opening Spanish questions, to indicate uncertainty.
  3. It is forbidden to write...
    • lowercase letters and uppercase letters mixed inside abbreviations. They shall consist exclusively of lowercase letters or only uppercase letters.
    • endings of ordinal numbers after the numerical value.
