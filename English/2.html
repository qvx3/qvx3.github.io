2. Evidence

Typewriters are already out of use as stand-alone devices, while more and more types of electronic equipment are communicating with the user using a displayed text. Every day, approximately 36% world's population uses the Latin alphabet and its variations (according to WorldStandards.eu), but not every device is adapted to support text in Polish. The commonly used SMS system allows sending up to 160 characters covered by the seven-bit GSM standard. This number is reduced to 140 characters for a 8-bit coded message, i.e. it contains at least one letter within Polish alphabet. These changes in the spelling of the Polish language, which I propose to introduce by official means, admittedly increase the volume of the text, but limit the set of letters to twenty-six characters of the Latin alphabet without diacritized letters.

The ways of entering letters within Polish alphabet are varied among machines: the telephone keypad requires repeated pressing of a key to which three to four letters of the Latin alphabet have been assigned alternately with the letters of the Polish alphabet (for example, Nokia 2700 classic telephone) or omitting these Latin letters to enter one letter Polish (Samsung GT-E1200). Typing these letters on the on-screen keyboard of devices with a many times larger display (diagonal of the display used in Samsung SM-T235 tablet is 3.5 times larger than that of Nokia 2700 classic usually involves touching and holding a letter of the Latin alphabet, and then moving finger towards the corresponding diacritized letter. The first method was partly automated using the T9 dictionary, which constructs a word consisting of letters assigned to the keys pressed in turn only once. If the machine displays an undesirable word, use the * key to select another one, but consisting of letters placed under the same keys (Nokia 2700 classic). The combined or separate spelling of words depends only on the device's user – space is created by pressing 0. An improved dictionary is found in devices with a touch screen: after entering the text, above the keyboard appears a list of words formed from the word on which the cursor is placed, and other words with similar spelling (Samsung SM-T235) are displayed. Completing or correcting a word is done by selecting one from the list or touching the space bar, depending on the machine setting.
